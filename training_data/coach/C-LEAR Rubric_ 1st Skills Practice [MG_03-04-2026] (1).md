# **Purpose of the 1st Skills Practice**

The 1st Skills Practice evaluates the clinician’s ability to practice and apply individual components of the **C-LEAR communication approach** within a guided learning environment.

This scenario involves **Anne Palmer and her 10 year old daughter Riley**. The parent expresses concern that her child is too young for the HPV vaccine because she is not sexually active.

This guided practice focuses on the correct sequencing and use of the following **C-LEAR phases**:

1. Counsel  
2. Listen  
3. Empathize  
4. Answer  
5. Recommend

The goal of this practice is to reinforce the correct use of these communication skills through supportive coaching before participants progress to the more independent **2nd Skills Practice**.

# **Rubric Scoring Model**

The scoring model uses a scale from **0 to 1**.

| Score | Meaning |
| ----- | ----- |
| 1 | Effective skill use |
| 0.5 | Partial or incomplete skill use |
| 0 | Skill not used or used incorrectly |

These scores are used **only for internal detection and research purposes**.

Participants receive **qualitative coaching feedback only**.

---

# **Scoring to Feedback Mapping (Required Logic for AI Coach)**

Numeric scores guide feedback selection internally.

**Score \= 1**

Indicates effective skill use and triggers **Keep Doing** feedback that reinforces what the clinician did well.

**Score \= 0.5 or 0**

Indicates partial or missed skill use and triggers **Try Next Time** feedback that offers brief and actionable guidance for improvement in a future attempt.

Feedback must **never reference numeric scores, point values, or rubric language.**

# **C-LEAR Phases and Performance Criteria**

## **Phase 1 \- Counsel**

### **What We Look For**

The clinician introduces the HPV vaccine using a **clear endorsement statement** that includes **at least four of the following five elements**:

1. **Age Timing:** Mentions the appropriate vaccination age (around 9 to 12 years old).

2. **Cancer Prevention:** States that the HPV vaccine prevents cancer.

3. **Safety:** Communicates that the vaccine is safe.

4. **Professional Endorsement/Recommendation:** Indicates that the clinician or clinic recommends the vaccine.

5. **Two Dose Plan:** Mentions returning for the second dose.

### ***Counsel Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | ----- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Clear endorsement statement including at least four of the five elements  |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Statement includes one to three elements but misses important parts of the endorsement |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | No clear counsel statement shared  |

### 

### ***COUNSEL Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *“We have a vaccine for children around 9-10 years old that prevents against six types of cancer.  We recommend that \[your child\] get this safe vaccine today and come back in six months for a second dose.” “The HPV shot protects against cancer and is completely safe. We recommend starting it around age 9 or 10.”* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“The HPV vaccine is for 10 year olds and our office recommends your child get this safe vaccine.” “The HPV vaccine is really safe and there are few side effects. It’s not required for school but if they get it now, they only have to come back and get just one more dose, versus getting 3 shots when they’re older.”* |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“Your child is due for the HPV shot.” “Are you ready for your vaccines today?”* |

## **Phase 2 \- Listen**

### **What We Look For**

The clinician invites the parent to share more detail by using an “Explore” or “Restate”  Listen skill:

**Explore** – Invites the parent to explain their concern. 

* Examples:  
  * “Can you tell me more about what worries you?”   
  * “What concerns do you have?”  
  * “Can you tell me more about what makes you feel she might be too young?”  
  * “What concerns you about starting the vaccine at her age?”  
  * “Can you tell me more about your thoughts on the timing of the vaccine?”

**Restate** – Reflects  the parent concern without judgment.

* Examples:  
  * “It sounds like you’re wondering why she would need the vaccine at this age.”  
  * “It sounds like you feel she might be too young for the vaccine.”  
  * “So your concern is that she isn’t sexually active yet.”

### ***Listen Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Uses an “Explore” or “Restate” skill that prompts the parent to talk more about their specific concern. |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Uses another skill such as Acknowledge, Normalize, or Validate instead of Explore or Restate |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Does not use an “Explore” or “Restate” skill |

### ***LISTEN Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *“Can you tell me more about what makes you feel she might be too young?” “It sounds like you’re wondering why she would need this vaccine at her age.” “So you’re concerned that she isn’t sexually active yet, and you’re not sure why she would need it now.”* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | (Uses an Empathy skill instead of a Listen skill) *“I can see that you’re worried about the HPV shot”* (Acknowledge) *“Lots of parents have concerns about this vaccine”* (Normalize) *“It’s okay to have questions about the side effects of the HPV vaccine”* (Validate) |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | (Does not explore or restate) *“The vaccine is recommended at this age.”  “She is due for it today.”  “It’s safe and effective.”* |

## **Phase 3 \- Empathize**

### **What we look for**

The clinician responds to the parent’s concern using an **Empathy skill** before giving the Answer. Empathy skills include:

**Acknowledge** – Recognizes the parent’s concern

* Examples:  
  * “I can understand why you’d be concerned about that”   
  * “I can understand why you’d feel that way”  
  * “I can understand why you would wonder about starting the vaccine at her age.”

**Normalize** – Reassures the parent that others share similar concerns 

* Examples:  
  * “A lot of parents ask that same question”  
  * “Many parents ask this…”  
  * “Many parents ask that same question about why the vaccine is recommended this early.”

**Validate** – Affirms the parent’s effort or perspective 

* Examples:  
  * “I appreciate you looking into this”   
  * “That’s a legitimate concern”  
  * “That’s a really thoughtful question about the timing of the vaccine.”

NOTE: An empathy statement may appear alongside a listening skill (**Explore** or **Restate**). If an empathy response is clearly present, it should still be credited even when paired with a listening statement.

* Example of combined empathy and listening:  
  * “That’s a really thoughtful question. Can you tell me more about what makes you feel she might be too young for the vaccine?”

### 

### ***EMPATHIZE Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Uses an **Acknowledge**, **Normalize**, or **Validate** skill that clearly responds to the parent’s concern before giving the Answer |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Uses a generic statement such as “I understand,” or gives the Answer before responding to the concern |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | No empathy statement or dismisses the parent’s concern |

### ***EMPATHIZE Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *“I can understand why you’d wonder about starting the vaccine at her age.” “Many parents ask the same question about why it’s recommended before kids are older.” “That’s a really thoughtful question about timing, and I’m glad you brought it up.”* (Combined empathy \+ listening, still counts) *“I can understand why you’d ask that. Can you tell me more about what you’ve heard about the timing?”* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | (Generic empathy or empathy after answering) *“I understand.” “Okay.” “It’s fine.”* (Answer comes first, empathy comes later) *“The vaccine works best when given earlier. I understand your concern.”* |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | (No empathy or dismissive response) *“You don’t need to worry about that.” “That’s not really a concern.” “She is due for it, so we should just do it today.”* |

## **Phase 4:  Answer**

### **What we look for**

The clinician addresses the parent’s concern about **why the HPV vaccine is recommended at this age**.

The parent’s concern focuses on two ideas:

* the child is **too young**  
* the child is **not sexually active yet**

Effective answers should explain that:

* the vaccine works **better when given earlier**  
* the vaccine protects the child **before exposure to the virus**

### ***ANSWER Rubric for 1st Skills Practice***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | ----- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Answers the parent concern by covering both key ideas:   Vaccine works better when given earlier                                                                     Vaccine protects the child before they are exposed                                                |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Addresses **one key idea** or the answer is unclear |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Does not answer the concern or provides inaccurate information |

### ***ANSWER Example Statements for Scenario 1***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *“We give it early because kids’ immune systems respond better at this age, and they are protected long before they become sexually active.”* *“The vaccine protects your child long before they are exposed to the virus and it’s more effective at a younger age.” “The vaccine works best when it’s given before someone is exposed to HPV, which is why we start it at this age.”* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“She may not be sexually active now, but someday she will be.”* *“The vaccine is more effective when we give it at this age.” “Don’t worry, the vaccine is totally safe.”*  |

## **Phase 5 \- Recommend**

### **What we look for**

The clinician provides a **clear recommendation immediately after the Answer.**

* Examples of recommendation language:  
  * “I recommend…”  
  * “I strongly recommend…”  
  * “We recommend…”  
  * “Our clinic recommends…”

The recommendation should **follow directly after the explanation**.

This approach reinforces the communication structure of the C-LEAR framework.

### ***Recommend Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | ----- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Clear recommendation delivered immediately after the Answer |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Recommendation present but not placed directly after the Answer |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | No recommendation or clinician defers without explanation |

### 

### ***RECOMMEND Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *“We give it early because kids’ immune systems respond better now, and they are protected long before they become sexually active \- that’s why I strongly recommend your child get the vaccine”*  |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“We recommend the HPV vaccine at this age because it works better earlier.”* (The recommendation appears within the explanation rather than clearly concluding the response.)                  |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“No problem, your child can wait and get the shot next year.”* |
