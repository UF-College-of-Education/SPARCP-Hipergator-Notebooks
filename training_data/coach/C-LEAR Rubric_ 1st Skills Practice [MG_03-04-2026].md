# **Purpose of the 1st Skills Practice**

The 1st Skills Practice evaluates the clinician’s ability to practice and apply individual components of the **C-LEAR communication approach** within a guided learning environment.

This scenario involves **Anne Palmer and her 10 year old daughter Riley**. The parent expresses concern that her child is too young for the HPV vaccine because she is not sexually active.

This guided practice focuses on the correct sequencing and use of the following **C-LEAR phases**:

1. Counsel  
2. Listen  
3. Empathize  
4. Answer  
5. Recommend

The goal of this practice is to reinforce the correct use of these communication skills through supportive coaching before participants progress to the more independent **2nd Skills Practice**.

• backend research data collection  
 • internal skill detection

Numeric scores must **never** be shared with participants, referenced in feedback, or implied in any way during the skills practice experience.

# **Rubric Scoring Model**

The scoring model uses a scale from **0 to 1**.

| Score | Meaning |
| ----- | ----- |
| 1 | Effective skill use |
| 0.5 | Partial or incomplete skill use |
| 0 | Skill not used or used incorrectly |

These scores are used **only for internal detection and research purposes**.

Participants receive **qualitative coaching feedback only**.

---

# **Scoring to Feedback Mapping (Required Logic for AI Coach)**

Numeric scores guide feedback selection internally.

**Score \= 1**

Indicates effective skill use and triggers **Keep Doing** feedback that reinforces what the clinician did well.

**Score \= 0.5 or 0**

Indicates partial or missed skill use and triggers **Try Next Time** feedback that offers brief and actionable guidance for improvement in a future attempt.

Feedback must **never reference numeric scores, point values, or rubric language.**

# **C-LEAR Phases and Performance Criteria**

## **Phase 1 \- Counsel**

### **What We Look For**

The clinician introduces the HPV vaccine using a **clear endorsement statement** that includes **at least four of the following five elements**:

1. **Age Timing:** Mentions the appropriate vaccination age (around 9 to 12 years old).

2. **Cancer Prevention:** States that the HPV vaccine prevents cancer.

3. **Safety:** Communicates that the vaccine is safe.

4. **Professional Endorsement/Recommendation:** Indicates that the clinician or clinic recommends the vaccine.

5. **Two Dose Plan:** Mentions returning for the second dose.

### ***Counsel Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | ----- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Clear endorsement statement including at least four of the five elements  |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Statement includes one to three elements but misses important parts of the endorsement |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | No clear counsel statement shared  |

### 

### ***COUNSEL Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *“We have a vaccine for children around 9-10 years old that prevents against six types of cancer.  We recommend that \[your child\] get this safe vaccine today and come back in six months for a second dose.” “The HPV shot protects against cancer and is completely safe. We recommend starting it around age 9 or 10.”* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“The HPV vaccine is for 10 year olds and our office recommends your child get this safe vaccine.” “The HPV vaccine is really safe and there are few side effects. It’s not required for school but if they get it now, they only have to come back and get just one more dose, versus getting 3 shots when they’re older.”* |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“Your child is due for the HPV shot.” “Are you ready for your vaccines today?”* |

## **Phase 2 \- Listen**

### **What We Look For**

The clinician invites the parent to share more detail by using an “Explore” or “Restate”  Listen skill:

**Explore** – Invites the parent to explain their concern. 

* Examples:  
  * “Can you tell me more about what worries you?”   
  * “What concerns do you have?”  
  * “Can you tell me more about what makes you feel she might be too young?”  
  * “What concerns you about starting the vaccine at her age?”  
  * “Can you tell me more about your thoughts on the timing of the vaccine?”

**Restate** – Reflects back the parent concern without judgment.

* Examples:  
  * “It sounds like you’re wondering why she would need the vaccine at this age.”  
  * “It sounds like you feel she might be too young for the vaccine.”  
  * “So your concern is that she isn’t sexually active yet.”

### ***Listen Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Uses an “Explore” or “Restate” skill that prompts the parent to talk more about their specific concern. |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Uses another skill such as Acknowledge, Normalize, or Validate instead of Explore or Restate |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Does not use an “Explore” or “Restate” skill |

### ***LISTEN Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *After the Counsel statement, the clinician listens to the parent’s response. Then they give a “Restate” skill: “It sounds like you have some concerns about the HPV vaccine.” or “It sounds like you’re worried about side effects.” After the Counsel statement, the clinician listens to the parent’s response. Then they use an “Explore” Listening skill: “What are your concerns?” or “What are you worried about?”* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *After the Counsel statement, the clinician listens to the parent’s response. Then they use another skill like “Acknowledge,” “Normalize” or “Validate,” but not an “Explore” or “Restate” Listening skill:  “I can see that you’re worried about the HPV shot” (Acknowledge) or “Lots of parents have concerns about this vaccine” (Normalize) or “It’s okay to have questions about the side effects of the HPV vaccine” (Validate)* |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *After the Counsel Statement, does not  give a “Restate” or “Explore” skill statement. After the Counsel Statement, interrupts or talks over the parent and does not give a “Restate” or “Explore” skill statement.* |

## **Phase 3 \- Empathize**

### **What we look for**

The clinician uses an “acknowledge,” “normalize,” and/or “validate” Empathy skill **before** giving the Answer. Empathy skills include:

**Acknowledge** – Recognizes the parent’s concern

* Examples:  
  * “I can understand why you’d be concerned about that”   
  * “I can understand why you’d feel that way”  
  * “I can understand why you would wonder about starting the vaccine at her age.”

**Normalize** – Reassures the parent that others share similar concerns 

* Examples:  
  * “A lot of parents ask that same question”  
  * “Many parents ask this…”  
  * “Many parents ask that same question about why the vaccine is recommended this early.”

**Validate** – Affirms the parent’s effort or perspective 

* Examples:  
  * “I appreciate you looking into this”   
  * “That’s a legitimate concern”  
  * “That’s a really thoughtful question about the timing of the vaccine.”

NOTE: An empathy statement may appear alongside a listening skill (**Explore** or **Restate**). If an empathy response is clearly present, it should still be credited even when paired with a listening statement.

* Example of combined empathy and listening:  
  * “That’s a really thoughtful question. Can you tell me more about what makes you feel she might be too young for the vaccine?”

### ***EMPATHIZE Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Uses an **Acknowledge**, **Normalize**, or **Validate** skill that clearly responds to the parent’s concern before giving the Answer |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Uses a generic statement such as “I understand,” or gives the Answer before responding to the concern |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | No empathy statement or dismisses the parent’s concern |

### ***EMPATHIZE Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *After the parent shares their concern, the clinician uses an “acknowledge” Empathy skill before giving the Answer: “That is a very valid question” or “I can understand why you’d want to be sure this is safe” or “I can see you care a lot about your child.”*  OR *After the parent shares their concern, the clinician uses a “validate” Empathy skill before giving the Answer:  “It makes sense you’d wonder why she needs it at her age” or  “That’s a really great question” or “It’s great that you’re wanting to know more about the safety of the vaccine.“* OR *After the parent shares their concern, the clinician uses a “validate” Empathy skill before giving the Answer:  “A lot of parents have that question” or “As a parent, I’d want to know the safety of the vaccine as well.”* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *Clinician uses a generic statement: “I understand.” or “I see.”*  OR *After the parent shares their concern, the clinician gives an Answer first BEFORE using an “acknowledge,” “normalize,” and/or “validate” Empathy skill: “We always recommend the vaccine at this age because it’s safe and prevents disease. I can understand why you’d be concerned.” (Acknowledge)* OR *After the parent shares their concern, the clinician gives an Answer first,  and then follows by using  a “Restate” or “Explore” Listening skill instead of an “acknowledge,” “normalize,” and/or “validate” Empathy skill:  “The vaccine is totally safe and has little side effects. What concerns do you have?” (Explore) or  “The HPV vaccine protects your child before they are exposed to disease, so it’s important they get it today. It sounds like you are worried about side effects?” (Restate)* |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *After the parent shares their concern, the clinician does not use an “acknowledge,” “normalize,” and/or “validate” Empathy skill. Instead, they skip the Empathy skill and go straight to an Answer: Doctor \- “The HPV vaccine is safe and there are few side effects.” Parent \- “I guess I’m still not comfortable with that.”  Doctor \- “I can give you some literature about the vaccine and you can think about it.”*                                                                       OR  *After the parent shares their concern, the clinician does not use an “acknowledge,” “normalize,” and/or “validate” Empathy skill.  Instead, they dismiss the parent’s concern: “You really shouldn’t listen to conspiracy theories online since this vaccine is safe”  or  “It’s totally up to you, she can wait until next year then”*  |

## **Phase 4:  Answer**

### **What we look for**

The clinician addresses the parent’s concern about **why the HPV vaccine is recommended at this age**.

The parent’s concern focuses on two ideas:

* the child is **too young**  
* the child is **not sexually active yet**

Effective answers should explain that:

* the vaccine works **better when given earlier**  
* the vaccine protects the child **before exposure to the virus**

### ***ANSWER Rubric for 1st Skills Practice***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | ----- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Answers the parent concern by covering both following points:   Vaccine works better when given earlier                                                                     Vaccine protects the child before they are exposed                                                |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Answers the parent concern by covering only one of the two points above  OR Answer is vague (unclear) |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Does not answer or gives inaccurate information (flag) |

### ***ANSWER Example Statements for Scenario 1***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *“We give it early because kids’ immune systems respond better now, and they are protected long before they become sexually active”*  or  *“The vaccine protects your child long before they are exposed to the virus and it’s more effective at a younger age.”* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“I know your child isn’t having sex yet, but someday they will be.”*  or  *“The vaccine is more effective when we give it at this age.”* or  *“Don’t worry, the vaccine is totally safe.” \[vague answer example\]* |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“The HPV shot isn’t mandatory for school so it’s not necessary.”* |

### ***Scoring-to-Feedback Mapping (Required Logic)***

Numeric scores directly guide the **type of coaching feedback** the AI Coach provides. This mapping must be applied consistently across all phases of the 1st Skills Practice.

* **Score of 1**  
   Indicates effective skill use.  
   → Triggers **“Keep Doing” feedback only**, reinforcing what the clinician did well.

* **Score of 0.5 or below**  
   Indicates partial, delayed, out-of-order, or missed skill use.  
   → Triggers **“Try Next Time” feedback**, offering brief, specific, and actionable guidance on how to improve in a future attempt.

Numeric values are **never** referenced in feedback language. The participant should not be aware that numeric scoring is occurring.

## **5\) Recommend immediately and clearly (Weight 1\)**

### ***What we look for***

* The clinician provides a strong, clear recommendation using: “I recommend,” “I strongly recommend,” “We recommend,” “The clinic recommends,” or “We strongly suggest”  
* The recommendation follows directly after the Answer (“punctuation” strategy).

**NOTE:** For feedback purposes, the participant will complete the **4\) Answer** and the **5\) Recommend** phases together in the training roleplay and booster roleplay. The coach will share the combined  **4\) Answer** and the **5\) Recommend** feedback once the participant has finished the **5\) Recommend** phase.

### ***Recommend Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | ----- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Clinician uses a clear Recommend skill (e.g. “I strongly recommend,” “We recommend,” “The clinic recommends,” “I strongly recommend,” “We strongly suggest”) delivered immediately after the Answer. |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Clinician uses a correct recommend skill, but it wasn’t directly after the Answer. (e.g., it could be before Answer, or during the Empathize phase)  |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Clinician does not use a Recommend skill, or they defer without medical rationale. |

### 

### ***RECOMMEND Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | The clinician first answers the parent's concern, and then directly transitions to their recommendation:  *“We give it early because kids’ immune systems respond better now, and they are protected long before they become sexually active \- that’s why I strongly recommend your child get the vaccine”*  or  *“There’s no evidence that the vaccine affects fertility or pregnancy, which is why our clinic strongly suggests she get it.”*  |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | EITHER: Does not recommend or strongly suggest the shot after answering the parent’s question the first time, but later recommends it after the second chance:                                                 *Parent \- “She’s only 10, so why is it needed?” (1st chance)                                               Doctor \- “Your child is protected better at an earlier age.”         Parent \- “But she’s not having sex yet…” (2nd chance)                                            Doctor \- “Your child is protected long before they become sexually active \- that’s why I strongly recommend your child get the vaccine.”* OR: Recommends or strongly suggests the shot after answering the parent’s question the first time, but uses the recommend skill at the beginning instead of the end of Answer, or uses it during another part of the C-LEAR approach:                                              *Parent \- “She’s only 10, so why is it needed?” (1st chance)                                               Doctor \- “We recommend your child get the vaccine at this age because she is protected better at an earlier age”   (Does not end Answer with the recommendation)*                       |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | No recommendation  or says *“No problem, your child can wait and get the shot next year.”* |
