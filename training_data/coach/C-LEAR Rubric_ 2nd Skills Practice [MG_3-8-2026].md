# **Purpose of the 2nd Skills Practice**

The 2nd Skills Practice evaluates the clinician’s ability to independently conduct a complete **C-LEAR conversation** without prompts or structured guidance.

This scenario involves **Maya Pena and her 9-year-old daughter Luna**. The parent expresses concern about **serious side effects of the HPV vaccine, specifically infertility**.

This independent practice focuses on the clinician’s ability to apply the full sequence of the **C-LEAR communication phases**:

Counsel

Listen

Empathize

Answer

Recommend

The goal of this practice is to assess how clinicians apply these communication skills during a realistic clinical interaction.

---

# **Purpose of Scoring**

Numeric scores are used only for:

* backend research data collection

* internal skill detection

Numeric scores must **never be shared with participants**, referenced in feedback, or implied in any way during the skills practice experience.

Participants should experience this session as **qualitative coaching**, not evaluation.

---

# **Rubric Scoring Model**

The scoring model uses a scale from **0 to 1**.

| Score | Meaning |
| ----- | ----- |
| 1 | Effective skill use |
| 0.5 | Partial or incomplete skill use |
| 0 | Skill not used or used incorrectly |

These scores are used **only for internal detection and research purposes**.

Participants receive **qualitative coaching feedback only**.

---

# **Scoring-to-Feedback Mapping (Required Logic for AI Coach)**

Numeric scores guide feedback selection internally.

Score \= 1  
 Triggers **Keep Doing** feedback reinforcing what the clinician did well.

Score \= 0.5 or 0  
 Triggers **Try Next Time** feedback offering brief, actionable guidance for improvement.

Feedback must **never reference numeric scores, point values, or rubric language**.

---

# **Communication Skill Cross-Credit Rule**

If the clinician uses a communication skill from a different **C-LEAR phase**, partial credit may be awarded.

This reflects appropriate communication effort while reinforcing the intended C-LEAR phase sequence.

Examples:

* Using an **Empathy skill** instead of a Listen skill may receive partial credit during the **Listen phase**.  
* Using a **Listen skill** instead of an Empathy skill may receive partial credit during the **Empathize phase**.

# **C-LEAR Phases and Performance Criteria**

## **Phase 1 \- Counsel**

### ***What we look for***

The clinician introduces the HPV vaccine using a **clear endorsement statement** that includes **at least four of the following five elements**:

1. **Age Timing:** Mentions the appropriate vaccination age (around 9 to 12 years old).

2. **Cancer Prevention:** States that the HPV vaccine prevents cancer.

3. **Safety:** Communicates that the vaccine is safe.

4. **Professional Endorsement/Recommendation:** Indicates that the clinician or clinic recommends the vaccine.

5. **Two Dose Plan:** Mentions returning for the second dose.

### ***Counsel Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | ----- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Clear endorsement statement including at least four of the five elements.  |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Statement includes one to three elements but misses important parts of the endorsement. |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | No clear counsel statement shared. |

### 

### ***COUNSEL Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *“We have a vaccine for 9-to-10- year olds that prevents against six types of cancer.  We recommend that Luna get this safe vaccine today and return in six months for a second dose.” “The HPV shot protects against cancer and is completely safe. We recommend it as young as 9-10 years old.”* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“The HPV vaccine is for 10 year olds and our office recommends your child get this safe vaccine.” “The HPV vaccine is really safe and there are few side effects. It’s not required for school but if they get it now, they only have to come back and get just one more dose, versus getting 3 shots when they’re older.”* |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“Luna is due for the HPV shot.” “Are you ready for your vaccines?”* |

## **Phase 2 \- Listen**

### ***What We Look For***

The clinician invites the parent to share more detail by using an “Explore” or “Restate”  Listen skill:

**Explore** – Invites the parent to explain their concern. 

* Examples:  
  * “Can you tell me more about what concerns you about the vaccine?”  
  * “What have you heard about the HPV vaccine?”  
  * “Can you tell me more about what worries you about side effects?”

**Restate** – Reflects the parent concern without judgment.

* Examples:  
  * “It sounds like you’re worried about serious side effects.”  
  * “So your concern is that the vaccine might affect fertility.”

### ***Listen Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Uses an “Explore” or “Restate” skill that prompts the parent to talk more about their specific concern. |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Uses another skill such as Acknowledge, Normalize, or Validate instead of Explore or Restate |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Does not use an “Explore” or “Restate” skill |

### ***LISTEN Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *“Can you tell me more about what you’ve heard about the HPV vaccine?” “It sounds like you’re worried the vaccine might cause serious side effects.”* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *(Empathy used instead of Listening) “I can see that you’re worried about the vaccine.” (Acknowledge) “A lot of parents have concerns about vaccine safety.” (Normalize) “That’s an understandable concern.” (Validate) These responses show empathy but **do not invite the parent to elaborate**, which is the goal of the Listen phase.* |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *Immediately answering without exploring the concern. “The vaccine is safe and recommended.”* |

## **Phase 3 \- Empathize**

### ***What we look for***

The clinician responds to the parent’s concern using an **Empathy skill** before giving the Answer. Empathy skills include:

**Acknowledge** – Recognizes the parent’s concern

* Examples:  
  * “I can understand why you’d be concerned about that.”  
  * “I can see why that would worry you.”  
    

**Normalize** – Reassures the parent that others share similar concerns 

* Examples:  
  * “A lot of parents ask that same question.”  
  * “Many parents have questions about vaccine safety.”

**Validate** – Affirms the parent’s effort or perspective 

* Examples:  
  * “That’s a really important question.”  
  * “I’m glad you brought that up.”

### ***EMPATHIZE Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Uses an **Acknowledge**, **Normalize**, or **Validate** skill that clearly responds to the parent’s concern before giving the Answer |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Uses a generic statement such as “I understand,” or gives the Answer before responding to the concern |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | No empathy statement or dismisses the parent’s concern |

### ***EMPATHIZE Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *“I can understand why you’d want to be sure the vaccine is safe.” (Acknowledge) “A lot of parents ask that same question about fertility.” (Normalize) “That’s an important question to ask about your child’s health.” (Validate)* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *Listen skill instead of empathy: “Can you tell me more about what you’ve heard about infertility?” Or Empathy skill placed after the Answer.* |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“I can give you some literature about the vaccine and you can think about it.”  “You shouldn’t worry about that.” “It’s totally up to you, she can wait until next year then”*  |

## **Phase 4 \- Answer**

### ***What we look for***

The clinician clearly addresses the parent’s concern that the HPV vaccine may cause **serious side effects, specifically infertility**.

An effective answer should include both of the following points:

1. The HPV vaccine has been **extensively studied and shown to be safe**, with over 15 years of research and hundreds of millions of doses given worldwide.

2. There is **no evidence that the HPV vaccine affects fertility or pregnancy**.

### 

### ***ANSWER Rubric for Scenario 2 (“I’ve heard there are a lot of side effects for the HPV vaccine”)***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | ----- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Answers the parent concern by covering both of the following points:  It has been shown to be safe/ It has been given for over 15 years and/or over 500 million doses There is no evidence that it leads to infertility/affects fertility |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Addresses the concern but includes only one key point or the answer is vague |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Does not answer the concern or provides inaccurate information |

### ***ANSWER Example Statements for Scenario 2***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *“The HPV vaccine has been studied for more than 15 years with over 500 million doses given worldwide. There’s no evidence that it affects fertility or pregnancy”*   *“The HPV vaccine does not  affect fertility and pregnancy, and it has been widely studied for over 15 years and totally safe.”* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“There’s no evidence that the vaccine affects fertility”*  *“This vaccine is safe and has been given over millions of doses.”  “I’ve never heard of that side effect.” \[vague answer\]* |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“You can skip the vaccine for now.”* |

## **Phase 5 \- Recommend**

### ***What we look for***

After answering the parent’s concern, the clinician provides a **clear recommendation** for vaccination **immediately after the Answer.**

* Examples of recommendation language:  
  * “I recommend…”  
  * “I strongly recommend…”  
  * “We recommend…”  
  * “Our clinic recommends…”

The recommendation should **follow directly after the explanation**.

This approach reinforces the communication structure of the C-LEAR framework.

### 

### ***Recommend Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | ----- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Clear recommendation delivered immediately after the Answer |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Recommendation present but not placed directly after the Answer |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | No recommendation or clinician defers without explanation |

### 

### ***RECOMMEND Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *“We give it early because kids’ immune systems respond better now, and they are protected long before they become sexually active \- that’s why I strongly recommend your child get the vaccine”*  *“There’s no evidence that the vaccine affects fertility or pregnancy, which is why our clinic strongly suggests she get it.”*  |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“It’s up to you, but I do recommend the vaccine.”* |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“No problem, your child can wait and get the shot next year.”* |
