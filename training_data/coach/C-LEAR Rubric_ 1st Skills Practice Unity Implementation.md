# **1st Skills Practice Rubric Purpose and Use**

The 1st Skills Practice evaluates the clinician’s ability to practice and apply individual components of the C-LEAR approach within a guided, formative learning environment.

This scenario involves Anne Palmer and her 10-year-old daughter Riley. The parent expresses concern that her child is too young for the HPV vaccine because she is young, and is not sexually active.

Scoring reflects the clinician’s use of each phase of the C-LEAR approach, with structured feedback designed to reinforce sequencing, clarify skill application, and support skill development before progressing to the 2nd Skills Practice.

---

# **Purpose of Scoring and Use of Numeric Values**

Numeric scores in this rubric are used **only** for backend research data collection and internal skill detection. Numeric scores must **never** be shared with the participant, referenced in feedback, or implied in any way during the skills practice experience.

Participants should experience this session as **qualitative, formative coaching**, not evaluation.

---

# **Rubric Scoring Model**

The scoring model is based on a scale from 0 to 1, with 0 being the lowest score and 1 being the highest score.

The numeric scores will not be shared with the participant/user.  They are only used for backend data collection.

**Prohibited in All Coach Feedback**

* Numeric scores  
* Fractions or point values (e.g., “half credit”)  
* Rubric language such as “out of order” or “incorrect”

Feedback must remain qualitative, supportive, and framed as coaching for future improvement.

---

# **Scoring-to-Feedback Mapping (Required Logic for AI Coach)**

Numeric scores are used internally to guide feedback selection and for research data collection only.

* A score of **1** indicates effective skill use and triggers **“Keep Doing” feedback only**, reinforcing what the clinician did well.

* A score of **0.5 or below** indicates partial or missed skill use and triggers **“Try Next Time” feedback**, paired with brief, actionable guidance on how to improve in a future attempt.

* Feedback should never reference numeric scores, performance levels, or point values.

# The user should experience feedback as qualitative, supportive coaching, not evaluation.

## **1\) Counsel the vaccine benefits and plan (Weight 1\)**

### ***Completion Criteria***

The clinician introduces the HPV vaccine using a clear endorsement statement that includes at least 4 out of the 5 key elements: 

1. The child’s age (9-12 range appropriate)  
2. that the vaccine prevents against six types of cancer,   
3. it is safe,   
4. it is recommended, and   
5. that they will come back for a second dose.

### ***Counsel Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | ----- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Complete statement covering at least 4 of the 5 elements. |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Statement done incorrectly, only covering 1 to 3 of the elements; misses two or more key points |
| **0 \[NOT THERE YET\]** | Not done. No clear counsel statement shared.  |

### 

### ***COUNSEL Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Covers all 5 elements: *“We have a vaccine for 9-to-10- year olds that prevents against six types of cancer.  We recommend that \[your child\] get this safe vaccine today and come back in six months for a second dose.”* Covers at least 4 of the 5 elements: *“The HPV shot protects against cancer and is completely safe. We recommend it as young as 9-10 years old.”* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Covers only 1-3  elements: *“The HPV vaccine is for 10 year olds and our office recommends your child get this safe vaccine.” “The HPV vaccine is really safe and there are few side effects. ## **2\) Listen (Weight 1\)**

### ***What We Look For***

* The clinician invites the parent to share more detail by using an “Explore” or “Restate”  Listening skill:

  * **Explore** – Invite more detail (e.g. “Can you tell me more about what worries you?”, “Can you tell me more about that?”, or “What concerns do you have?”)

  * **Restate** – Reflect back the parent concern without judgment (e.g. “So your main concern is the side effects…”, “It sounds like you’re worried about side effects…”)

### ***Listen Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Uses an “Explore” or “Restate” Listening skill that prompts the parent to talk more about their specific concern. |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Uses another skill, such as “acknowledge,” “normalize,” and/or “validate” instead of using an “Explore” or “Restate” Listening skill.  |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Does not use an “Explore” or “Restate” Listening skill |

### ***LISTEN Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *After the Counsel statement, the clinician listens to the parent’s response. Then they give a “Restate” skill: “It sounds like you have some concerns about the HPV vaccine.” or “It sounds like you’re worried about side effects.” After the Counsel statement, the clinician listens to the parent’s response. Then they use an “Explore” Listening skill: “What are your concerns?” or “What are you worried about?”* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *After the Counsel statement, the clinician listens to the parent’s response. Then they use another skill like “Acknowledge,” “Normalize” or “Validate,” but not an “Explore” or “Restate” Listening skill:  “I can see that you’re worried about the HPV shot” (Acknowledge) or “Lots of parents have concerns about this vaccine” (Normalize) or “It’s okay to have questions about the side effects of the HPV vaccine” (Validate)* |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *After the Counsel Statement, does not  give a “Restate” or “Explore” skill statement. After the Counsel Statement, interrupts or talks over the parent and does not give a “Restate” or “Explore” skill statement.* |

## **3\) Empathize – Acknowledge, Normalize, Validate (Weight 1\)**

### ***What we look for***

* The clinician uses an “acknowledge,” “normalize,” and/or “validate” Empathy skill **before** the Answer: 

  * **Acknowledge** – Recognize the caregiver concern (e.g. “I can understand why you’d be concerned about that”, “I can understand why you’d feel that way”)

  * **Normalize** – Reassure that others share this concern (e.g. “A lot of parents ask that same question”, “Many parents ask this”)

  * **Validate** – Affirm the caregiver’s effort or perspective (e.g. “It’s great that you’ve been reading about this”, “I appreciate you looking into this,” “That’s a legitimate concern.”)

### ***EMPATHIZE Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Uses at least one targeted “acknowledge,” “normalize,” and/or “validate” Empathy skill matched to the specific parent’s concern BEFORE giving the answer; smooth transition to Answer. |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | EITHER: Uses a generic statement (e.g. “I understand”) instead of using an Empathy skill that directly acknowledges, validates, or normalizes the parent’s concern;  OR: Uses an “explore” or “restate” skill OR: Gives the Answer first before using an Empathy skill that directly acknowledges, validates, or normalizes the parent’s concern |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | EITHER: Does not use an “acknowledge,” “normalize,” and/or “validate” Empathy skill OR:  Dismisses the parent’s concern. |

### ***EMPATHIZE Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *After the parent shares their concern, the clinician uses an “acknowledge” Empathy skill before giving the Answer: “That is a very valid question” or “I can understand why you’d want to be sure this is safe” or “I can see you care a lot about your child.”*  OR *After the parent shares their concern, the clinician uses a “validate” Empathy skill before giving the Answer:  “It makes sense you’d wonder why she needs it at her age” or  “That’s a really great question” or “It’s great that you’re wanting to know more about the safety of the vaccine.“* OR *After the parent shares their concern, the clinician uses a “validate” Empathy skill before giving the Answer:  “A lot of parents have that question” or “As a parent, I’d want to know the safety of the vaccine as well.”* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *Clinician uses a generic statement: “I understand.” or “I see.”*  OR *After the parent shares their concern, the clinician gives an Answer first BEFORE using an “acknowledge,” “normalize,” and/or “validate” Empathy skill: “We always recommend the vaccine at this age because it’s safe and prevents disease. I can understand why you’d be concerned.” (Acknowledge)* OR *After the parent shares their concern, the clinician gives an Answer first,  and then follows by using  a “Restate” or “Explore” Listening skill instead of an “acknowledge,” “normalize,” and/or “validate” Empathy skill:  “The vaccine is totally safe and has little side effects. What concerns do you have?” (Explore) or  “The HPV vaccine protects your child before they are exposed to disease, so it’s important they get it today. It sounds like you are worried about side effects?” (Restate)* |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *After the parent shares their concern, the clinician does not use an “acknowledge,” “normalize,” and/or “validate” Empathy skill. Instead, they skip the Empathy skill and go straight to an Answer: Doctor \- “The HPV vaccine is safe and there are few side effects.” Parent \- “I guess I’m still not comfortable with that.”  Doctor \- “I can give you some literature about the vaccine and you can think about it.”*                                                                       OR  *After the parent shares their concern, the clinician does not use an “acknowledge,” “normalize,” and/or “validate” Empathy skill.  Instead, they dismiss the parent’s concern: “You really shouldn’t listen to conspiracy theories online since this vaccine is safe”  or  “It’s totally up to you, she can wait until next year then”*  |

## **4\) Answer – Address the Concern Accurately and Clearly (Weight 1\)**

### ***What we look for***

* The clinician directly addresses the stated Scenario 1 parent concern: timing issue \- their child’s age is too young for this vaccine  
  * *Why give it to 9- to 12-year-olds?*

  * ### *My child isn’t having sex yet, so why is it needed?*

**NOTE:** For feedback purposes, the participant will complete the **4\) Answer** and the **5\) Recommend** phases together in the 1st skills practice.  The coach will share the combined  **4\) Answer** and the **5\) Recommend** feedback once the participant has finished the **5\) Recommend** phase.

### ***ANSWER Rubric for Scenario 1*** 

### *(“My child isn’t having sex yet, so why is it needed?”)*

| Level | Description |
| ----- | ----- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Answers the parent concern by covering both following points:   Vaccine works better when given earlier                                                                     Vaccine protects the child before they are exposed                                                |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Answers the parent concern by covering only one of the two points above  OR Answer is vague (unclear) |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Does not answer or gives inaccurate information (flag) |

### ***ANSWER Example Statements for Scenario 1***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | *“We give it early because kids’ immune systems respond better now, and they are protected long before they become sexually active”*  or  *“The vaccine protects your child long before they are exposed to the virus and it’s more effective at a younger age.”* |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“I know your child isn’t having sex yet, but someday they will be.”*  or  *“The vaccine is more effective when we give it at this age.”* or  *“Don’t worry, the vaccine is totally safe.” \[vague answer example\]* |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | *“The HPV shot isn’t mandatory for school so it’s not necessary.”* |

## **5\) Recommend immediately and clearly (Weight 1\)**

### ***What we look for***

* The clinician provides a strong, clear recommendation using: “I recommend,” “I strongly recommend,” “We recommend,” “The clinic recommends,” or “We strongly suggest”  
* The recommendation follows directly after the Answer (“punctuation” strategy).

**NOTE:** For feedback purposes, the participant will complete the **4\) Answer** and the **5\) Recommend** phases together in the training roleplay and booster roleplay. The coach will share the combined  **4\) Answer** and the **5\) Recommend** feedback once the participant has finished the **5\) Recommend** phase.

### ***Recommend Rubric***

| Level | Description |
| ----- | ----- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | Clinician uses a clear Recommend skill (e.g. “I strongly recommend,” “We recommend,” “The clinic recommends,” “I strongly recommend,” “We strongly suggest”) delivered immediately after the Answer. |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Clinician uses a correct recommend skill, but it wasn’t directly after the Answer. (e.g., it could be before Answer) |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | Clinician does not use a Recommend skill, or they defer without medical rationale. |

### 

### ***RECOMMEND Example Statements***

| Level | *EXAMPLES* |
| ----- | :---- |
| **1 \[KEEP DOING\]** | The clinician first answers the parent's concern, and then directly transitions to their recommendation:  *“We give it early because kids’ immune systems respond better now, and they are protected long before they become sexually active \- that’s why I strongly recommend your child get the vaccine”*  or  *“There’s no evidence that the vaccine affects fertility or pregnancy, which is why our clinic strongly suggests she get it.”*  |
| **0.5 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | EITHER: Does not recommend or strongly suggest the shot after answering the parent’s question the first time, but later recommends it after the second chance:                                                 *Parent \- “She’s only 10, so why is it needed?” (1st chance)                                               Doctor \- “Your child is protected better at an earlier age.”         Parent \- “But she’s not having sex yet…” (2nd chance)                                            Doctor \- “Your child is protected long before they become sexually active \- that’s why I strongly recommend your child get the vaccine.”* OR: Recommends or strongly suggests the shot after answering the parent’s question the first time, but uses the recommend skill at the beginning instead of the end of Answer, or uses it during another part of the C-LEAR approach:                                              *Parent \- “She’s only 10, so why is it needed?” (1st chance)                                               Doctor \- “We recommend your child get the vaccine at this age because she is protected better at an earlier age”   (Does not end Answer with the recommendation)*                       |
| **0 \[TRY NEXT TIME\]** | No recommendation  or says *“No problem, your child can wait and get the shot next year.”* |
